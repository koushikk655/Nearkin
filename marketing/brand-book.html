01 · Logo construction

Two circles. Perfect math.

The symbol is two overlapping circles of identical diameter, separated by 40% of their diameter, with their centers on a horizontal axis. The left circle is the canvas-contrast color (cream on dark, charcoal on light). The right circle is saffron #FF8A3D, blended via "screen" on dark or "multiply" on light.

Symbol shape 2 circles, identical radius
Circle diameter (relative) 0.6 of bounding box width
Horizontal separation 0.4 of bounding box width
Vertical alignment Centers on horizontal axis
Left circle (dark canvas) #F5F0E6 cream
Left circle (light canvas) #1A1A1A charcoal
Right circle #FF8A3D saffron
Right circle blend mode screen (dark) / multiply (light)
Wordmark Fraunces 300 lowercase
Wordmark letter-spacing -0.04em
Symbol-to-wordmark gap 25% of symbol height

03 · Logo misuse

What not to do.

The logo is a precise object. Every deviation below is a brand violation — the symbol's geometry, the wordmark's typography, and the saffron accent are all locked.

CORRECT
neario
Cream + saffron on charcoal
The canonical dark-canvas lockup. Both circles visible, wordmark in cream.
WRONG
neario
Reversed circles
The saffron circle is always on the right. Don't flip them — the geometry is the brand.
WRONG
neario
Color substitution
Saffron #FF8A3D is the only correct accent. Never replace with blue, green, or any other brand color.
WRONG
NEARIO
Wordmark in the wrong font
The wordmark is always Fraunces 300 lowercase. Never Inter, never uppercase, never bold.
WRONG
neario
Rotated / skewed
The symbol is always horizontal — centers on the X axis. No rotation, no skew, no perspective.
WRONG
neario
Placed on a busy / colored background
Use the logo only on Neario's canvas colors (charcoal #0E0E0E or cream #F5F0E6) or over photography with appropriate dark-tint backdrop for legibility.

06 · Voice & tone

How we sound.

Neario writes like a thoughtful friend who runs the local boutique. Direct, warm, specific. Never bro-y, never corporate, never bossy. Concrete numbers beat vague promises.

→ WE SAY
  • Real shops, real makers, real close.
  • Shop your locality.
  • The kirana on your street, the pickle aunty next door.
  • Your order arrives in 28 minutes
  • 156 followers · 4.8 stars · open now
  • Anita just confirmed your order
  • 3.2 km away in your locality
  • Paid weekly via UPI, straight to your bank
× WE DON'T SAY
  • The best hyperlocal commerce experience
  • Crafted with love and passion
  • Lightning-fast delivery, guaranteed
  • Authentic, traditional, homemade goodness
  • Join 1 million happy customers
  • Become a part of our family
  • Get amazing deals on everything
  • Experience the magic of local
TONE PRINCIPLE 01
Specific over vague
"28 minutes" not "fast delivery". "156 followers" not "lots of fans". "₹360 box of 12" not "great price".
TONE PRINCIPLE 02
Lowercase over title-case
"home-made" not "Home-Made". "shop your locality" not "Shop Your Locality". Editorial restraint extends to capitalization.
TONE PRINCIPLE 03
First-name over corporate-speak
"Anita confirmed your order" not "Your order has been confirmed by the seller". Sellers have names.
07 · Photography

What Neario looks like.

Photography is documentary, not staged. Warm natural light over flash. Hands at work over polished product shots. Showing the maker over showing the product alone. The aesthetic is editorial — Bon Appetit, Aesop, Kinfolk — not e-commerce.

CORRECT
Natural light, hands at work
Warm window light from the side. The maker's hands centered, the product secondary. The story is human-led, not product-led.
WRONG
Harsh flash, oversaturated
Direct flash flattens the warmth. High-contrast / cold-tinted product photography reads as e-commerce, not editorial. Avoid.
→ DO
  • Window light, golden hour, side-lit shadows
  • Cream linen, dark slate, marble — natural surfaces
  • Hands kneading, piping, arranging — process shots
  • Shallow depth of field, intentional blur
  • Warm tones · cream, amber, saffron, deep brown
× DON'T
  • On-camera flash, overhead studio light
  • White seamless backdrops (catalog-style)
  • Faces / smiling people (stock-feeling)
  • Over-saturated colors, HDR, filters
  • Cool tones · cyan, magenta, neon, electric
