a small manifesto

We don't believe in "hyperlocal" as a marketing word.

We believe in the bakery on your street that doesn't have an app. The pickle aunty whose orders happen on WhatsApp. The craft maker selling DM-to-DM on Instagram. The corner sweet shop that has been there forty years and still uses a paper notebook.

Neario is for them. And for you — the person who lives nearby and would much rather buy from a real shop run by a real person than from a faceless catalog.

We're not trying to replace the shops next door. We're trying to put them in your pocket, exactly as they are.

how it works, for buyers

Three taps. Or none, if you're a regular.

First,

you open the app and Neario knows where you are.

No category lists, no city pickers, no faff. Just shops near you that are open right now — sorted by what's closest and who you've ordered from before.

Then,

you pick what you want and how to pay.

UPI or Razorpay if you like things fast. Cash on delivery if you'd rather pay when they arrive. No platform-mandated discounts. No surge.

Finally,

they bring it to your door, themselves.

Sellers deliver their own orders within 5 km. Mostly on a scooter, sometimes on foot, occasionally by their teenage cousin. The seller's name is on the order. You can message them.
